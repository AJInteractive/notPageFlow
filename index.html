On the Streets of Catania

Lamin (21) from Gambia, left his country looking for a better life. The journey in the Sahara lasted 10 days. He traveled with other 31 people inside a Jeep. Unfortunately the car had an accident, it overturned and one person died. After be hiding for some months in Tripoli, fearful of the stay in Libya, he decided to cross the sea towards Sicily. So, after paying a sum, he started the trip by boat. After some hours the boat started to sink. Everybody inside the boat tried to throw out the water with any tool, even with the hands. Fortunately the italian navy helped them soon. Contrariwise, the second boat who was traveling together with them, had a big problem and sinked. At least 45 people died in that shipwreck. In this tragedy he lost his uncle and his cousin. Now that he is in Italy he wants to stay and learn italian very soon in order to increase the opportunity to find a good job and try to restart his life again.

On the Streets of Catania

Samate Madu (18) from Caramance region, Senegal. Fled after rebels tried to draft him into their ranks. Trekked to Mali, on trucks, smuggling trails, etc, for two months. Then on to Agadez, Niger where he spent weeks doing tailoring work to pay the next stage. Then to Libya. Finally squeezed on to a smuggler boat with 100 people. The Italian Coast Guard rescued them after seven hours at sea. An ardent AC Milan soccer fan, he dreams of becoming a footballer. Asked what his plans are in Europe, he says, "My mother is back in the village. I have to help her."

On the Streets of Pozzallo

Bamba Ji (19) in Gambia has been a student with a great passion for football. Immediately after breakfast or at the end of the school day, always went to play football.His journey by boat to reach the coast of Sicily has been tremendous. In the rescue operations made by a cargo ship that has them intercepted in the middle of the mediterranean sea, in going up the stairs, due to the general panic, his close friend with whom he had started the trip fell down and drowned. Bamba Ji has an uncle in Germany, but his desire, once obtained the documents, would be to go to Switzerland and groped to play in any team as a professional.
